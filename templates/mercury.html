Mercury


History, Common Uses, and Fun Facts

Mercury is a heavy liquid metal, which is also toxic. Due to its toxicity, many uses of mercury are being phased out or are under review. It is now mainly used in the chemical industry as catalysts. It is also used in some electrical switches and rectifiers. Previously its major use was in the manufacture of sodium hydroxide and chlorine by electrolysis of brine. These plants will all be phased out by 2020. It was also commonly used in batteries, fluorescent lights, felt production, thermometers and barometers. Again, these uses have been phased out. Mercury easily forms alloys, called amalgams, with other metals such as gold, silver and tin. The ease with which it amalgamates with gold made it useful in recovering gold from its ores. Mercury amalgams were also used in dental fillings. Cinnabar (aka vermilion, mercury sulfide, HgS), was used as a bright red pigment by the Palaeolithic painters of 30,000 years ago to decorate caves in Spain and France. Cinnabar would yield up its mercury simply on heating in a crucible, and the metal fascinated people because it was a liquid that would dissolve gold. The ancients used in on a large scale to extract alluvial gold from the sediment of rivers. The mercury dissolved the gold which could be reclaimed by distilling off the mercury. Although highly toxic, mercury had many uses, as in thermometers, but these are now strictly curtained.


Safety Concerns

Chemical Contact Acute Health Effect First Aid Measures
Eye Contact Redness, pain, and watery eyes. Flush eyes with water for 10 minutes. If irritation or pain persists, see a doctor.
Inhalation Breathing difficulty, coughing, pneumonia, and pulmonary edema. Can be fatal! Relocate to fresh air. If breathing is difficult, trained personnel should administer emergency oxygen.
Skin Contact Redness, dry skin, and pain. Wash skin gently and thoroughly with water and non-abrasive soap. Call a Poison Centre or doctor.
Ingestion Nausea, vomiting, central nervious system effects, and damage to the kidneys. Rinse mouth with water. Immediately call a Poison Centre or doctor.
